    I’m a senior data scientist at Hawkfish, Mike Bloomberg’s political tech and data organization, and have spent the last year working to defeat Donald Trump and win back the senate for the Democrats.
    Prior to my career as a data scientist, I spent nine years working in venture capital building seed-stage companies in the biofuels, water treatment, radioactive waste remediation, mining, and sustainable packaging industries.
    I’m active in a number of organizations throughout New York City, including the New York Road Runners where I’m a member of the executive committee of the board of directors, chair of the development committee, and vice-chair of the public affairs committee, the Central Park Track Club, of which I was president from 2008 to 2017 and currently serve on the board, and the Brooklyn Bridge Park Conservancy, where I’m also a board member.
    I was a competitive runner back in the day and was the first Brooklyn resident and 46th overall finisher in the 2011 New York City Marathon, finishing in 2:29:46. That was a long time ago and now I get winded chasing after my kids. I live with my wife and two boys in Brooklyn.

    Example Projects

    • Bayesian Hypothesis Testing API As part of the Direct Response Experiments team for the Mike Bloomberg 2020 Campaign, I built our hypothesis testing tool using a Bayesian approach. The team deployed sets of ads in a Randomized Controlled Trial setting and wanted to know which ad(s) drove the highest rates of conversion (of clicks, email submission, form completion, etc.). The tool modeled conversion rates using the beta distribution, enabling fast Bayesian updating, and producing results that were easy to interpret by a broad audience (e.g. ‘probability of being best’ versus p-values). I deployed the API on kubernetes using flask.
